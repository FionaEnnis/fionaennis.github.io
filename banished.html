Work
Classical with a twist


This project proposal for the Royal Irish Academy of Music, was for the Irish premiere of Stephen McNeff’s Banished, an adaption of Steve Gooch’s play, Female Transport, to a libretto by Olivia Fuchs. Based on the true story of the first female prisoners transported to Australia from the UK and Ireland in the early 19th century, Banished tells the story of how a group of people join to overcome their differences and survive adversity. After reading the libretto,
I found there were five main themes within the opera: tension, journey, violence, rebel and escape.





After extended experimentation, I began using the sheet music from the opera, initially to represent the unsettled sea these women travelled, which then extended into representing the mentally unsettling journey they made. For the poster, I explored the themes of tension and rebellion and visualized this through the subversion of classical sheet music and the vicious cuts throughout it. Tension is also found in the contrast between the classical sheet music and contemporary san serif.





The booklet design follows the poster with references to tension and the classical sheet music. It follows a music book format, similar to the typographic composition of the poster. The booklet is designed in a classical way, with subtle references to sheet music compositions, such as music scales, but with a contemporary twist. The layering of the poster depicts the upper and lower decks within the prison ship. At closer inspection, details about the opera can be found within the sheet music as if to replace the lyrics. The impactful poster exhibits the tension between the gentile music notes and the aggressive cuts, which embodies the tension between the upper and lower decks.
