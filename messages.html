Doing the messages


In this self directed project, I began by recalling the lost shopping list I had previously found on the ground and had pinned to my bedroom wall. I began exploring the value I instantly found in this mundane object and the possible lost story behind it. I created several questions and posed them to the people around me, wondering what story they would create around the found list and the lost character who wrote it. I then commenced scavenging supermarket tills, carparks and trolleys for these little lost stories. I collected an extensive amount of data, by asking 10 different subjects, 10 questions on each shopping list selected.












The stripped back, sterile aesthetic enhances the beauty of the humble shopping lists, and; exaggerates the function of the mundane object in the book. The tone of this case study is investigative; facts are discovered about the found lists, and, stories are created around the lost characters. The title “The Messages” draws on the colloquial phrase ‘going for the messages’ meaning going to do the shopping. The title also reflects the hidden meanings and stories that are uncovered by the subjects. This case study brought attention to a simple, everyday object — the shopping list. Most importantly, this research project was successful in further understanding the perception of everyday objects while focusing on the ‘lost’ individuals who wrote each of these shopping lists.
